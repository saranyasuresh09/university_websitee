The Native American History
Of Eagle

The education of Native Americans is woven into the long history of
Harvard University. The Charter of 1650, which continues to govern
Harvard, pledges the University to “the education of English and
Indian youth.”
From 1655 to 1698, the “Indian College” stood in Harvard Yard, on
the site currently occupied by Matthews Hall.
It was not until 1970 that a program was established to specifically
address Native American issues. In early 1970, the American Indian
Program emerged on campus.
